Virtually Paint Your Room Before Buying a Drop of Paint

Choosing a wall color is a big commitment. Paint Color Visualizer lets you photograph your room and digitally apply any color from any major paint brand directly to your walls — so you see the result before you spend anything.

How virtual room painting works

Take a photo of the room you want to repaint — or use one from your camera roll. Tap the wall area you want to recolor. The app uses AI to detect wall surfaces in the image and applies your chosen color to the wall pixels, adjusting for the room's existing lighting and shadow patterns to produce a realistic result.

The recoloring happens on your device in real time — no uploading to a server, no waiting. Tap a different paint color and the wall updates immediately.

Choosing from thousands of real brand colors

Browse or search the complete color libraries of Sherwin-Williams, Benjamin Moore, Behr, Valspar, Farrow & Ball, and more. Every color in the app is a real product you can buy at a paint store — not an approximation or a generic color.

Filter by color family (blues, greens, neutrals, whites), brand, or search by name. When you find a color you want to test, tap it and see it on your wall immediately.

Testing multiple colors on different walls

Paint different walls in the same photo with different colors to test accent wall ideas, two-tone schemes, or coordinating colors for adjacent walls. Each wall surface can be assigned a different color independently.

Save multiple versions of the same room with different color schemes and compare them side by side before making a decision. This is faster and cheaper than buying multiple sample cans and painting test patches.

Sharing your virtual paint result

Export the recolored room photo and share it via email, Messages, or social media. The share image shows your room with the new color applied at full quality — useful for getting a second opinion from a partner, designer, or contractor before committing.

All saved room visualizations are stored in the app with the color name and brand so you can return to any saved option later.
